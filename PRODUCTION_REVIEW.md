# Production Review

This note records the comparison used for the production pass.

## Reference Sites

- The Batch by DeepLearning.AI: editorial AI briefing with a clear promise, newsletter habit, and topic-driven reading.
- The Rundown AI: short daily AI briefing positioned around speed and subscriptions.
- TLDR AI: concise technical newsletter with clear categorization and source-heavy scanning.
- Ledge.ai / AIsmiley: Japanese AI media patterns, including categories, search, rankings, and business-oriented navigation.

## Gaps Found

- The old name, AI暦, did not clearly communicate the value of the site.
- There was no search or topic filtering, which makes archives hard to use once articles grow.
- The site lacked RSS, sitemap, robots.txt, and structured metadata for production discovery.
- The editorial policy was not visible, so readers had little reason to trust summaries.
- Source links existed, but they did not feel like a first-class part of the reading experience.
- There was no lightweight sharing path for an article.

## Changes Made

- Renamed the product to AI Nexus Daily.
- Added search, tag filtering, reading time, and share controls.
- Added RSS, sitemap, robots.txt generation.
- Added Open Graph, Twitter card, canonical URL, and JSON-LD metadata.
- Added editorial policy and production-ready RSS/update CTA sections.
- Kept the static Cloudflare Pages architecture intact.
